## Subjects, Actions, and Scene Context

### Subject types

- People by role or archetype (e.g., stoic knight, weary detective), age, wardrobe, era
- Animals (breed specificity), mythical/fantastical
- Objects (hero prop, vehicle type, device), abstract forms
- Multiple subjects (ensemble blocking; interactions; spatial relationships)

### Actions and beats

- Basic movement: walking, running, turning, looking, rising, collapsing
- Interactions: conversing, arguing, embracing, handing over, revealing
- Expressions: confident, uneasy, devastated, resolute
- Subtle cues: hair ruffles, swallow, micro-smile, blinking
- Transformations/process: blooming, melting, construction montage, decay

### Scene and context

- Interior/exterior, time of day (dawn, golden hour, blue hour, night), weather (overcast, drizzle, thunderstorm, fog, snow)
- Locale: cyberpunk alley, medieval courtyard, sterile lab, desert plateau
- Atmospherics: dust motes in beams, wet street reflections, heat haze, floating pollen
- Era/world: 1920s jazz club, 1980s arcade, near-future lab, alien desert
